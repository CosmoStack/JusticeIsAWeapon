1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Justice is a Weapon is a 2.5D detective mystery adventure for Android (primary) and Windows (secondary) where players investigate crimes using a supernatural ability to observe past events—at the cost of their eyesight. Unlike traditional detective games where clues are freely available, every piece of information comes with a permanent consequence. Players must strategically decide how much of their vision they're willing to sacrifice to solve each case.

The game targets mystery enthusiasts aged 16+ and employs a hybrid monetization model (free-to-try with premium unlock) with significant DLC expansion potential. Built on Unity 6.3 LTS + URP, the single-player offline experience is technically achievable while offering a fresh take on the genre.

2. GAME CONCEPT & VISION

2.1 Core Idea

"The truth is out there. But how much are you willing to lose to find it?"

The player assumes the role of a world-renowned private detective possessing a unique supernatural ability: the power to observe past events. When investigating a crime, the detective can travel back to key moments surrounding the incident and witness events unfold in real-time.

THE CATCH: Every use of this power permanently damages the detective's eyesight.

The player must carefully balance gathering enough evidence to solve the case while preserving their vision long enough to make the correct accusation. Go blind too early, and you'll never see the full picture. But hesitate to use your power, and the killer walks free.

2.2 Vision Statement

Traditional detective games often focus on collecting clues without meaningful consequences. Players can investigate endlessly until the solution becomes obvious—eliminating tension and reducing deduction to a checklist.

Justice is a Weapon reimagines investigation by making information itself a limited resource. Every attempt to uncover additional evidence comes at a permanent cost. Players must decide whether the risk of losing their vision is worth gaining another clue.

2.3 Philosophical Foundation

  • Sacrifice: How much are you willing to give up for truth?
  • Obsession: The line between justice and self-destruction
  • Consequence: Every choice has a cost, even passive ones
  • Trust: Can you trust your own perception as it degrades?

4. CORE MECHANICS DEEP DIVE

4.1 Investigation Phase

Present Day Investigation

The player arrives at a crime scene in the present and gathers initial information through:

  • A. Environment Examination — Tap interactive objects, read documents, photograph scenes, discover physical evidence.
  • B. Witness Interviews — Question witnesses, each has 2-3 statements, contradictions, body language cues.
  • C. Police Reports — Case files, autopsy reports, timeline, suspect list.

4.2 Time Vision Mechanic

Activation Process

  1. Player selects a location or object at the crime scene
  2. Detective focuses their ability
  3. Screen effect: Color drain, temporal distortion
  4. Player witnesses the past event
  5. Vision degradation occurs immediately after viewing
  6. One major clue is revealed

Four Observation Stages

StageInformation Gathered
1. Before the CrimeVictim's routine, location state, potential motives
2. Crime PreparationCulprit's actions, tools gathered, planning evidence
3. Crime ExecutionThe act itself (perpetrator obscured)
4. Aftermath & WitnessesReactions, cover-up attempts, witnesses fleeing

Note: The killer's identity is always hidden through masks, shadows, or visual distortions—preventing players from instantly solving the mystery. This ensures deduction remains essential.

5. GAMEPLAY WALKTHROUGH

5.1 Complete Case Flow

Step 1: Crime Scene Arrival

  • Player arrives at the crime scene
  • Briefing from police contact
  • Initial environment scan
  • 5 suspects introduced
  • 2 initial clues available (no cost)

Step 2: Present Day Investigation

  • Examine environment (tap interactive objects)
  • Interview witnesses (3 available)
  • Review police reports
  • Collect 1 additional physical clue
  • Total clues: 3/5 required

Step 3: Time Vision (First Use — Free)

  • Focus on location of interest
  • Observe the "Before the Crime" stage
  • Witness critical detail
  • Vision remains clear
  • Gain clue, total: 4/5

Step 4: Risk Decision Point

  • Do you use another vision? Cost: permanent vision degradation
  • Alternative: Deduce with current clues
  • Risk: Wrong accusation or blind before solving

Step 5: Additional Investigation

OPTION A: Use Time Vision (Cost) — Observe "Crime Preparation", see suspect, vision degrades, gain clue.

OPTION B: Don't use vision — Review existing clues, cross-reference, attempt deduction with 4/5 clues.

Step 6: Deduction Phase

  • Review all collected clues
  • Review suspect alibis
  • Identify contradictions
  • Make deduction

Step 7: Accusation & Verdict

  • Select the suspect you believe committed the crime
  • Present evidence to support accusation
  • Receives one of five verdicts — from Perfect Justice to Wrongful Accusation (see Section 6 for full definitions)

6. MAIN FEATURES & USP

6.1 Unique Selling Points

1. Vision Sacrifice Mechanic

  • Information costs vision — every clue degrades your sight
  • Permanent consequences — no way to restore vision
  • Psychological tension — "Should I risk another look?"

2. Unique Time Investigation

  • Observe, don't change — no altering the past, only witnessing it
  • 4 crime stages — Before, Preparation, Execution, Aftermath
  • Hidden identity — killer always obscured, requiring deduction

3. Difficulty-Based Risk Scaling

DifficultyRisk LevelAppeal
EasyLowCasual mystery fans, first-time players
NormalMediumStandard detective challenge
HardHighExperienced deduction players
ExpertExtremeTrue mystery masters

4. Multiple Future Detectives

  • Each with unique ability and unique drawback
  • Different gameplay strategies required
  • Replay existing cases with new mechanics

5. No Combat Gameplay

  • Entire experience focuses on deduction and investigation
  • Accessible to narrative game fans
  • Unique in the action-heavy mobile market

6. Dynamic Mystery Solving

  • Solve cases with different amounts of evidence
  • Multiple valid deduction paths
  • No "right" way to play

8.3 Market Gap Analysis

What's Missing in Current Detective Games:

  1. Consequences for investigation — No game punishes you for gathering clues
  2. Tension beyond the story — Risk isn't just "who did it"
  3. Replayability through difficulty — Different clue requirements change strategy
  4. Mobile-first detective experiences — Few serious detective games on Android

Our Solution:

  • Vision sacrifice creates consequences
  • Every choice has tension
  • Difficulty changes gameplay significantly
  • Built specifically for mobile touch controls

11. AUDIO DESIGN

"Sound is the final sense to go. When you're blind, audio becomes your only window to the truth."

11.1 Philosophy

Audio in Justice is a Weapon is not decoration — it is information. As the player's vision degrades, the soundscape must be rich enough to carry the investigation. Every audio decision serves function first, atmosphere second.

11.3 Audio Mood Board

INVESTIGATION PHASE ───────────────────────────────────────── Instrumentation : Muted trumpet, upright bass, brushed snare Tempo : 60–75 BPM (slow, deliberate) Key/Mode : Minor (Dm, Bm) — melancholy but alert Dynamics : Low-volume ambient base; swells when clue found Feel : Rain-slicked streets, cigarette smoke, cold case files TIME VISION PHASE ───────────────────────────────────────── Instrumentation : Reversed piano, pitch-shifted cello, tape delay FX Tempo : No fixed tempo — follows scene pacing Key/Mode : Atonal / modal (Phrygian) Dynamics : Starts low, peaks at key reveal, cuts abruptly Feel : Watching memories through broken glass BLIND PHASE ───────────────────────────────────────── Instrumentation : SILENCE + spatial SFX only Logic : Footsteps, breathing, ambient hum used as navigation aids Audio cues : High-pitched chime = clue nearby; low rumble = suspect Feel : Sound becomes the only truth left

11.4 Sound Identity Keywords

Every audio decision should be evaluated against these four words: Cold. Deliberate. Fragile. Earned.

  • Cold — the world is not warm; the palette and music agree
  • Deliberate — no unnecessary cues; every sound carries meaning
  • Fragile — as vision degrades, so does the score's fullness
  • Earned — the orchestral resolution only hits hard because the silence before it was real

14. EXPANDABILITY & LIVE OPERATIONS

14.1 Three-Pillar Expansion Framework

The current roadmap has been restructured around three independent expansion pillars. Each pillar can grow without waiting for the others — content releases don't block each other.

14.1b Expandability Concept: New Gameplay Dimensions

The original roadmap added cases and detectives but didn't introduce new types of gameplay. The following are concrete expansion concepts that each add a genuinely different experience — not just more of the same.

Expansion ConceptWhat's NewTarget Phase
Verdict Tribunal Mode Instead of accusing one person, the player must assemble a case file — selecting the right clues, a motive, and a method. Wrong clue selections lead to acquittal. Changes the endgame from a single accusation to a "build the case" puzzle. DLC 2 (Full Release)
Witness Corruption System Witnesses can be bribed or scared into silence. Some interviews lock after too many questions. Forces players to prioritize who they interrogate first, adding social resource management. DLC 2 (Hard difficulty)
Multi-Location Cases A single case spans two linked crime scenes. Time Vision charges carry over between locations but degrade faster. Requires travel decisions — some clues are exclusive to each site. DLC 3 "Foresight"
Cold Case Mode Cases from decades ago. Time Vision has a maximum of 2 uses due to the detective's worsening condition. Evidence has degraded — some clues are missing or corrupted. Higher mystery, lower information. DLC 3 (Hard/Expert)
Co-Investigator Mode (2 Players) One player handles Time Vision; the other handles physical investigation and witnesses. Vision charges are shared — coordination required. Each player sees partial information and must communicate. Year 2 Cooperative DLC
Villain Arc — "The Copycat" A cross-case narrative where a recurring criminal appears across 5 cases. Solving each case unlocks a piece of the villain's identity. Culminates in an Expert-only final case with no vision charges available at all. Year 2 Story Campaign
Community Case Editor Players design and publish their own cases. Includes: suspect creator, dialogue tree editor, clue placement, time vision stage scripting. Featured cases get highlighted in a weekly "Community Spotlight." Procedural DLC
Seasonal Themes Each Battle Pass season reskins the noir world into a new aesthetic — e.g., 1920s Prohibition, Cold War espionage, near-future cyberpunk. Art and audio change; core mechanics remain identical. Keeps the game visually fresh without a redesign. Ongoing (Live Ops)

19. GLOSSARY

This section defines all core entities and terms used throughout the design document, for clarity across team members and evaluators.

TermDefinition
Time VisionThe detective's supernatural ability to observe past events at a crime scene location. Each use costs one permanent Vision Charge. The core risk/reward mechanic of the game.
Vision ChargeA single use of the Time Vision ability. Charges are consumed permanently — they do not regenerate during a case. Starting charges vary by difficulty.
Vision DegradationThe progressive loss of visual clarity that occurs with each Time Vision use. Tracked in five stages: Clear → Minor Blur → Moderate → Severe → Blind.
Blind PhaseThe game state in which the player has exhausted all Vision Charges and the screen goes dark. Navigation is audio-only. The case can still be solved from this state with sufficient prior clues.
CaseA single self-contained crime investigation. Each case has a unique victim, location, set of suspects, clues, and a definitive killer. Cases take 15–25 minutes to complete.
SuspectA character present in a case who may or may not be the killer. Every suspect has a name, role, motive, and alibi. Suspects vary in number by difficulty (5–12).
ClueA piece of evidence collected during investigation. Clues are either Physical (free, from crime scene) or Vision Clues (cost a Vision Charge). Each case requires a minimum number of clues to support an accusation.
AccusationThe final act of a case: the player selects a suspect and presents their case. Results in one of five possible endings.
DetectiveThe player-controlled character. The MVP features one detective ("The Visionary"). Future DLCs add new detectives, each with a unique ability and a different cost mechanic.
The VisionaryDetective 1. The MVP protagonist. Ability: Time Vision. Drawback: Vision Degradation per use. Archetype: Brooding, methodical, driven by obsession with truth.
The ListenerDetective 2 (DLC 1). Ability: Hear past conversations at a location. Drawback: Hearing degrades — later audio is muffled and unclear.
The PremonitionistDetective 3 (DLC 2). Ability: View brief flashes of the near future at a location. Drawback: Each premonition causes a headache, reducing available investigation time.
The EmpathDetective 4 (DLC 4 Campaign). Ability: Feel the emotional state of anyone in a location at a given time. Drawback: Emotional overload causes memory loss — clues can be forgotten.
VerdictThe outcome of an Accusation. Five possible verdicts: Perfect Justice, Justice Served, Partial Justice, Wrongful Accusation, Unsolvable.
DifficultyOne of four game modes (Easy / Normal / Hard / Expert) that controls suspect count, required clues, Vision Charge limit, and whether false clues are present.
DLC (Downloadable Content)A paid post-launch content pack adding new cases, detectives, or features. Priced between $1.99–$4.99.
Battle PassA seasonal (8-week) optional content subscription. Provides cosmetic rewards and one bonus case per season. Never gates core gameplay.
Clue PackA purchasable consumable providing additional Vision Charges for a single case. Marks the run as "Assisted" in statistics. Optional — never required.
Procedural ModeA post-launch game mode generating cases algorithmically from content pools. Provides theoretically infinite replayability. Released as DLC 3.
Case File / JournalThe in-game menu where players review collected clues, suspect notes, and evidence. Accessible at any point during investigation.
Crime SceneThe primary investigation location for a case. Contains interactive objects, witness characters, and Time Vision hotspots.
Time StageOne of four observable periods in a past event: Before the Crime / Crime Preparation / Crime Execution / Aftermath. Each stage reveals different information.
MVP (Minimum Viable Product)The initial release build: 5 cases, 1 detective, Easy/Normal difficulty, offline single-player.
Open BetaThe first public release on Google Play, offering Case 1 free and soliciting community feedback before Full Release.
Unity 6.3 LTS + URPThe game engine used for development. LTS = Long-Term Support (stable). URP = Universal Render Pipeline (mobile-optimized graphics).

20.3 Work Division Principle

  • Members with strong active participation own the critical path: Core Systems, UI Framework, and Case Development.
  • Members with moderate participation own scoped sub-tasks: individual case writing, specific screens, or asset categories.
  • Members with limited participation receive bounded tasks with clear scope: QA checklists, writing one case's suspect dialogue, or specific asset types.
